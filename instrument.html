    Piano (aka. Pianoforte)


    A piano is a keyboard musical instrument that has wire strings that sound when struck by felt-covered hammers. It has 88 keys. Of the three pedals found on most pianos, the damper pedal on the right lifts all the felt dampers above the strings, allowing them all to vibrate freely; the left pedal shifts the keyboard and action sideways to enable the hammer to strike only one of the two or three unison strings of each tenor and treble key (the bass notes are only single-strung); and the middle pedal (generally available on grand pianos but also found on some upright pianos) usually holds up the dampers only of those keys depressed when the pedal is depressed.


    Here are some types of pianos:

    Studio Piano

    Studio pianos are between 45” and 47” tall. These are a popular choice for schools and churches.

    Upright Piano

    Upright pianos are generally 48” and over. These pianos are the choice of players looking for grand piano type sound without taking up as much space.

    Grand Piano

    Grand pianos generally offer more sound projection than vertical pianos.

    Ukulele


    The ukulele is a four stringed instrument which looks more or less like a miniature classical guitar. The smallest kind is the soprano ukulele. The soprano ukulele offers the classic ukulele sound that most people would associate with the Hawaiian sound, but it may be difficult to play for those who have larger hands. There are then also the alto, tenor and baritone ukuleles, which increase in size with each one up to the baritone.

    Violin

    A violin is a four-stringed instrument that is played with a bow. The highest pitched and smallest instrument in its family, the violin has proven versatility in a wide range of musical styles. At the same time, the sweet and subtle sound it is able to create, it’s a favourite among concert halls and orchestras alike.



    What do you need to play the violin?

    Shoulder Rest

    This is used to make the violin comfortable to play for long hours.

    Rosin

    This is used to coat the bow hairs with a substance that will "grip" the strings.

    Tuner

    This is used to tune the violin and is usually attached to the violin pegs.
